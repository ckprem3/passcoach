What are passwords?

In a narrower IT concept a password can be defined as an array characters intended to differentiate an authorized entity from an unauthorized entity.
It is used for granting access to different resources for its holder.

Are your passwords safe?

A password can manifest multiple vulnerabilities:

  • Short or its complexity is weak (does not use upper, lower case, digits or special characters) makes it easily bruteforce-able
  • Prone to dictionary attacks, it is a common word and is pre/post fixed with a number or part of a known leak
  • Contains personal information such as your name, username or date of birth... makes it susceptible to social engineering
  • Using the same password in multiple accounts makes it a single point of failure for multiple resources, hacking your social media account can compromise your bank account too

General suggestions

Complexity

A strong password should be complex meaning length and search space are key-factors, should contain uppercase, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
A longer password is harder to crack. Ideally, a password should be at least 12 characters long.
Avoid commonly used phrases and dictionary words as they are easier to guess. Keep in mind the predictability of common transformations like password -> p4ssw0rd, also using only the first/last character as uppercase is also obvious

There is no full security

The absence of a hack doesn't prove unbreachability, it only shows that the resources needed for doing it outweighed the possible gains.
Therefore, change your passwords periodically. Think about it, if you change it in the middle of a bruteforce attack you just made the attackers waste their time and resources
Don't use the same password in multiple places once compromised, the probability to be guessed in other places increases exponentially. Use Multi-factor and biometric authentication these render offline attacks useless, which increases the effort needed by magnitudes.

Don't be a target

If an attacker doesn't know you, the reasons for attack are significantly smaller. The quieter you are the less probable that someone targets you.
If you are randomly selected for an attack there will be no extra effort spent on you vs anyone else. You don't have to be the fastest in the world just outrun the bottom 10 percentile.
If attackers have easier targets they will concentrate their efforts on those as they are promising a better ROI
