What are passwords?

In a narrower IT concept a password can be defined as an array characters intended to differentiate an authorized entity from an unauthorized entity.
It is used for granting access to different resources for its holder.

Are your passwords safe?

A password can manifest multiple vulnerabilities:

  • Short or its complexity is weak (does not use upper, lower case, digits or special characters) makes it easily bruteforce-able
  • Prone to dictionary attacks, it is a common word and is pre/post fixed with a number, part of a known leak
  • Contains personal information such as your name, username, or date of birth... makes it susceptible to social engineering
  • Using the same password in multiple accounts makes it single point of failure for multiple resources, hacking your social media account can compromise your bank account too

General suggestions

Complexity

A strong password should be complex, contain uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

A longer password is harder to crack. Ideally, a password should be at least 12 characters long.

Avoid commonly used phrases and dictionary words as they are easier to guess.
